Design Beginnings


Design Fuego

Logo A/B/C

I designed these three logos which I felt represented me as a student, creative, and person in general. I used Canva and Adobe Express.

UX Testing Logo Project

After conducting a study in which I interviewed three subjects about my designs A/B/C, I took their suggestions and revised one of my designed logos to fit better with what they thought worked..

Cartoon Portrait

I used the blob tool and the dropper tool in Adobe Illustrator order to trace a photo of myself, creating a cartoon. I utilized the layer panel to draw overtop of my image while still being able to hide the image from the cartoon.

Good Design

For this project, I chose to analyze the design principles represented in a Bjork poster. I used Adobe InDesign to create spotlights on parts of the poster.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

Rhetoric played a large role in my "What is Good Design" piece as I attempted to make my analyses easy to understand. I wanted my writing to sound professional without sounding stale or hard to understand. My descriptions of good design elements reflect rhetorical skills which I have learned through writing. Technology also played a huge role as I began working with Adobe applications. Adobe InDesign was used for this project and, in learning the tools in this application, I learned how to work with technology to make my designs look better. Sometimes, however, the technology wouldn't comply with exactly what I wanted in my design which made creation a bit frustrating sometimes. This, I feel, will only get better with practice.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

In my "What is Good Design" project, I utilized the design principles we have learned to create a visually cohesive and pleasing analysis of a poster. I used elements of alignment to align the text with their respective colored boxes, as well as within the entire design itself. I used the bright red of the title text and the stroke of the frames to create contrast with the otherwise black and white color scheme of the design. I made each frame the same size to create an element of consistency throughout the piece. Finally, I used what we have learned about typography in order to make my text easy to read, in accordance with the poster, and experimental.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

My "What is Good Design" project was most recently workshopped. I explained my process of creation for the design and opened myself up to critiques of my work. I listened to others' opinions about the elements of design which we have learned, including alignment, contrast, and proximity. I listened quietly while they told me how I could do better and attempted to talk through things I could change. I explained my thinking through revision and got feedback about the design looking better.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

After the workshop of my "What is Good Design" piece, I took the design back into InDesign and attempted to work through the comments I received from my peers. I worked with InDesign more, furthering my skillset in Adobe, and fixed a lot of the elements which, in my original draft, made the design hard to read, displeasing to the eye, confusing, etc. The piece which is shown in my portfolio reflects the revised piece after taking the comments I received from my peers.

5. Learn to present your work.

Similar to the "What is Good Design Piece," with the UX Design Study presentation, I learned how to make a visually pleasing and understandable Google Slides presentation. I took the instructions which were laid out in Canvas and modeled each slide after them. I practiced explaining my process of designing and my study to my classmates, and received questions about my presentation which helped better understand how I could do better in the future. I learned how to make my slides easy to read and learned to speak not only reading from the slides, but to have my own notes which I could read from.
